The Word of The Day is MAVERICK üí•

Definition: A maverick is a person who shows independence of thought and action, and refuses to conform to the norms of a group or society.

Etymology: The word maverick derives from the surname of a 19th-century Texan rancher, Samuel A. Maverick, who did not brand his cattle. As a result, any unbranded cattle became known as mavericks.

Example Sentences:

  1. Sheila's avant-garde fashion choices make her a true maverick in the industry.
  2. The politician's controversial stance on healthcare made her stand out as a maverick among her party members.
  3. The CEO's disruptive business strategies have solidified his reputation as a maverick in the corporate world.

Practice Tasks:

  • Write a short paragraph describing a person you know who embodies the spirit of a maverick.
  • Create a dialogue between two characters, one who is conventional and the other who is a maverick. Highlight their conflicting viewpoints.
  • Compose a poem using the word maverick in an expressive and creative way.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story where the protagonist, a maverick detective, solves a complex case using unconventional methods.

Drawing Task:

Create an imaginative drawing that represents the concept of a maverick breaking free from conformity.
