🤔 The Word of The Day is OBTUSE

Definition:

Obtuse means lacking quickness of perception or intellect. It refers to someone who is not easily understanding or noticing things.

Etymology:

The word obtuse originated from the Latin word obtusus, which means "blunted" or "dull."

Example Sentences:

  • I tried to explain the math problem to him, but he remained obtuse and couldn't grasp the concept.
  • Jessica's sarcastic remarks often go over people's heads because they are too obtuse to understand her humor.
  • The detective grew frustrated with the obtuse witness, who couldn't provide any useful information about the crime.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short story that includes at least two characters - one who is highly perceptive and another who is characteristically obtuse.
  2. Create three multiple-choice questions where the correct answer requires using the word obtuse in a sentence.
  3. Compose a poem using obtuse as your inspiration. The poem can be of any length or style you prefer.

Creative Writing Task:

Write an essay exploring the concept of being obtuse in today's society. Discuss how being less observant or lacking in intellectual acuity can affect individuals and their interactions with others.

Drawing Task:

Create a detailed sketch or drawing that represents the idea of obtuseness visually. Use your artistic skills to convey a scene or image related to the word's meaning.
