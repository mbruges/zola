The Word of The Day is 🧠 INNATE

Definition:

An adjective meaning existing from birth; inborn; inherent.

Etymology:

The word "innate" comes from the Latin word "innatus," which translates to "inborn." It was first used in the English language in the 15th century.

Example Sentences:

1. She had an innate talent for playing the piano.

2. The instinct to protect their young is innate in animals.

3. His innate curiosity led him to explore different cultures.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1:

Create a sentence using the word "innate" to describe a natural skill or ability you possess.

Task 2:

Write a short paragraph explaining why you think certain human emotions are innate.

Task 3:

Compose a sentence using "innate" to describe a characteristic that you believe everyone is born with.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story about a person who discovers an innate power or ability within themselves and how it affects their life.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that portrays something that is innate to you.
