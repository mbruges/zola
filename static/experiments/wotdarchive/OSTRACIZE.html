The Word of The Day is 🦆 OSTRACIZE

Definition:

Ostracize means to exclude, reject, or banish someone from a group or society.

Etymology:

The word "ostracize" comes from the ancient Greek word "ostrakizein," which means "to banish by voting with potsherds." In ancient Athens, citizens would write the name of the person they wanted to banish on a piece of broken pottery (ostrakon), hence the term.

Example Sentences:

  • I felt excluded and ostracized by my classmates because I was new in school.
  • After the scandal, the company decided to ostracize the dishonest employee.
  • If you continue to mistreat others, you may find yourself ostracized from the community.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1: Write a sentence using ostracize to describe a situation of social exclusion.

Task 2: Use ostracize in a sentence to explain how someone might be punished for their actions.

Task 3: Create a sentence using ostracize that suggests the consequences of continuously mistreating others.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem using ostracize as the central theme. Explore how exclusion affects the characters and the choices they make.

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture that represents the feeling of being ostracized or excluded. Use your imagination to illustrate the emotions associated with this experience.
