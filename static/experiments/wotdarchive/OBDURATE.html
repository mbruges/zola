The Word of The Day is OBDURATE 🤧

OBDURATE (adj.): Stubbornly refusing to change one's opinion or course of action; unyielding.

Etymology Summary:

Originating from Latin "obduratus" meaning "hardened", the word obdurate reflects the concept of being unyielding or stubborn in one's attitude or behavior.

Example Sentences:

1. Despite numerous pleas, he remained obdurate and refused to admit his mistake.

2. The politician's obdurate stance on the issue led to public outrage.

3. Even faced with overwhelming evidence, she remained obdurate in her belief.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1: Write a persuasive paragraph convincing your friend to change their obdurate opinion on a controversial topic.

Task 2: Create a dialogue between two characters, one firm in their resolve and the other trying to convince them to be less obdurate.

Task 3: Write a scenario where someone's obdurate behavior leads to negative consequences and discuss the lessons that can be learned from it.

Creative Writing Task:

Compose a story centered around a character who learns the value of flexibility and open-mindedness after encountering multiple obdurate individuals.

Drawing Task:

Illustrate a scene that depicts a person stubbornly resisting change, symbolizing the essence of being obdurate.
