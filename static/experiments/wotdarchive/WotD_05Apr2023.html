The Word of The Day is CIRCUMLOCUTION 😏

Circumlocution is a roundabout way of expressing something, often to the point of being unclear or evasive. 😕

The word comes from the Latin "circumlocutio," which means to speak in a roundabout way. This word is made up of "circum", meaning around, and "loqui", meaning to speak. 😮

Example sentences:

  1. She used so much circumlocution that I couldn't understand what she was trying to say. 🤔
  2. Politicians are notorious for their use of circumlocution when trying to avoid answering a straightforward question. 🙄

Practice Tasks:

  • Find and highlight the circumlocution in this passage.
  • Rewrite this sentence without using circumlocution.
  • Create a sentence using circumlocution that is difficult to understand.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or scene where one person is trying to get information from another who is intentionally using circumlocution to avoid answering. How does the conversation evolve?

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or cartoon of someone using circumlocution. What does it look like?
