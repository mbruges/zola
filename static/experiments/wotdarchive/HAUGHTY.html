Definition:

A term used to describe someone who is arrogant and excessively proud, often looking down upon others.

Etymology:

The word "haughty" originated from the Middle English word "haughti", meaning "high, lofty". It can be traced back to the Anglo-French word "haut", which means "high".

Example Sentences:

1. Despite her achievements, Emma's haughty attitude made it difficult for her to make friends.

2. The millionaire's haughty demeanor towards his employees created a tense work environment.

3. The queen's haughty behavior towards her subjects often caused resentment among the common people.

Practice Tasks:

1. Rewrite the following sentence using the word haughty: "The actress displayed a confident and lofty manner during her acceptance speech."
2. Use the word haughty in a sentence that describes someone's behavior at a social event.
3. Create a short dialogue between two characters in which one of them accuses the other of being haughty.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem inspired by the theme of haughtiness. Explore the consequences of such behavior and its impact on relationships and personal growth.

Drawing Task:

Create an original illustration that represents someone exhibiting a haughty attitude. Use your imagination and feel free to add captions or speech bubbles to enhance the illustration.
