The Word of The Day is PLETHORA 😏

Definition:

Plethora is a noun that means an excessive or overabundant amount or quantity.

Etymology:

The word plethora originates from the Late Latin word "pleth≈çra" which means "fullness" or "abundance". It entered the English language in the late 16th century.

Example Sentences:

1. After the holiday season, there was a plethora of unwanted gifts.

2. The buffet offered a plethora of options, leaving customers spoilt for choice.

3. The homeowner had a plethora of plants in their garden, creating a vibrant and lively space.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1: Write a sentence using the word plethora to describe a situation where there are too many options.

Task 2: Incorporate the word plethora into a sentence about an overflowing display of books in a library.

Task 3: Use the word plethora in a sentence describing an overwhelming amount of information presented in a lecture.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem using the word plethora at least three times. Be creative and enjoy exploring the nuances of the word.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the idea of plethora. It can be a visual representation of an abundance of anything you desire.
