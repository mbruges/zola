The Word of The Day is NOMINAL 🥳

Definition:

Nominal (adjective) - existing in name only; not real or actual; very small in amount or value; in name or form, but not in reality

Etymology Summary:

The word "nominal" originates from the Late Middle English word "nominalis", which means "pertaining to a name". It further evolved from the Latin word "nomen", meaning "name".

Example Sentences:

  1. The company charged a nominal fee for the service provided.
  2. His role in the project was merely nominal, as he did not contribute much.
  3. Despite his nominal title as manager, she was the one making all the important decisions.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph explaining a scenario in which a nominal fee is charged.
  2. Compose a sentence using nominal to describe something small or insignificant.
  3. Create a dialogue between two people using the word nominal to express something in name only.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a creative story that revolves around a character who lives a nominal life but craves extraordinary adventures and experiences.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that visually represents the concept of something being nominal. Use your imagination and artistic skills.
