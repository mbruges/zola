The Word of The Day is TURBULENCE 💥

Definition: a state of great disturbance, confusion, or uncertainty

Etymology: from Latin turbulentus, from turbo meaning "whirlwind, eddy"

Example Sentences:

  1. The turbulence on the airplane made some passengers feel uneasy.
  2. The turbulence in the stock market caused many investors to panic and sell their shares.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Use the word turbulence in a sentence.
  2. What are some synonyms for turbulence?
  3. What are some antonyms for turbulence?

Creative Writing Task: Write a short story (100-200 words) that involves the word turbulence. It can be about a turbulent airplane ride, a turbulent personal relationship, or any other scenario you come up with.

Drawing Task: Draw a picture of a turbulent scene, such as a stormy ocean or a tornado. Include the word turbulence in your drawing.
