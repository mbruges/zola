The Word of The Day is 🤮 MORBID

Morbid (adjective): suggesting an unhealthy mental state or attitude; gruesome or shocking in a way that is unsettling.

Etymology: The word "morbid" has its roots in the Latin word "morbidus," meaning diseased or sickly.

Example Sentences:

1. The morbid scenes in the horror movie left the audience feeling disturbed.

2. Sarah has a morbid fascination with true crime novels and documentaries.

3. The doctor grew concerned when she noticed a morbid fascination with death in her teenage patient.

Practice Tasks:

1. Rewrite the following sentence using the word morbid: "The sight of blood made him feel uneasy."

2. Fill in the blank with the appropriate form of morbid: "She has a _______ fascination with ghost stories."

3. In your own words, describe a situation that could be considered morbid.

Open-ended Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (at least 200 words) that explores the themes of mortality and morbid curiosity.

Drawing Task:

Create a visual representation of something that embodies the word morbid.
