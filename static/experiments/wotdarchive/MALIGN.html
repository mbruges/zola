The Word of The Day is 😈 MALIGN

Definition: Malaign means to speak harmful untruths about someone, or to slander or defame them.

Etymology: The word malign comes from the Old French word "malaignier" which means "to act maliciously."

Example Sentences:

1. She maligns her former colleagues by spreading false rumors about their work.

2. The tabloid newspaper continuously maligns celebrities in order to boost their sales.

3. The politician's opponents often malign her character in an attempt to discredit her.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph explaining a situation where someone might be tempted to malign another person.
  2. Create a dialogue between two people discussing the consequences of maligning someone's reputation.
  3. Compose an email advising a friend on how to handle false maligning statements being made about them.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem where the character's reputation is maligned by others, and how they overcome the challenges that arise because of it.

Drawing Task:

Create a visual representation of the word malign, showcasing its meaning and impact.
