The Word of The Day is 💩 MENDACIOUS

Definition: Not telling the truth; lying.

Etymology: The word "mendacious" originated from the Latin word "mendax", meaning "lying".

Example Sentences:

  1. Mendacious politicians often face consequences when their lies are exposed.
  2. He was known for his mendacious stories that deceived many.
  3. Her mendacious statement during the trial led to a mistrial being declared.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence using the word mendacious to describe a dishonest character in a book you've read.
  2. Compose a dialogue between two people where one accuses the other of being mendacious.
  3. Write a short paragraph on why it is essential to be truthful and avoid mendacious behavior in everyday life.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story exploring the consequences that a mendacious character faces when their lies catch up with them. How do their actions affect those around them?

Drawing Task:

Sketch a scene that represents the concept of mendacious behavior. Be creative!
