The Word of The Day is INFAMY 👀

Definition:

Infamy means having a bad reputation or being well-known for doing something harmful or disgraceful.

Etymology:

The word "infamy" originated from the Latin word "infamia" meaning "evil repute" or "ill fame".

Example Sentences:

  • The criminal was sentenced to a lifetime of infamy for his heinous crimes.
  • Despite their best efforts, the politicians couldn't escape the infamy attached to their corrupt practices.
  • The disastrous event brought infamy to the unsuspecting town.

Practice Tasks:

  • Create three sentences using the word infamy in different contexts.
  • Write a short story involving a character who strives to overcome their infamy.
  • Compose a poem that explores the theme of infamy and its consequences.

Creative Writing Task:

Write an essay discussing the impact of infamy on individuals and society. Explore its various forms and the possible ways to overcome it.

Drawing Task:

Create a visual representation of the word infamy. Use your imagination to portray its meaning through art.
