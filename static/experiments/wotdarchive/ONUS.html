The Word of The Day is ONUS 💪

Definition:

Onus is a noun that refers to the burden or responsibility of doing something.

Etymology:

The word "onus" comes from the Latin word "onus" which means "load" or "burden".

Example Sentences:

  • Onus is on you to complete the project by the end of the day.
  • The onus of proof lies with the prosecution in a criminal trial.
  • He voluntarily took the onus of organizing the charity event.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using the word onus in the context of a school assignment.
  2. Compose a paragraph explaining why it is important to understand the onus of personal finance.
  3. Create a dialogue between two characters discussing the onus of environmental sustainability.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (minimum 300 words) exploring a situation where the onus of saving the world rests on the shoulders of an unlikely hero.

Drawing Task:

Draw a visual representation of the word onus depicting a person carrying a heavy load.
