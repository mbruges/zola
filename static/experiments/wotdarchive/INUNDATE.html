The Word of The Day is INUNDATE 💨

INUNDATE: to overwhelm someone or something with a flood or abundance.

Etymology: The word "inundate" originates from the Latin word "inundare," which means "to overflow" or "to flood."

Example Sentences:

  1. The heavy rain caused the river to inundate the town.
  2. The news article was inundated with comments from angry readers.
  3. After the successful marketing campaign, our company was inundated with orders.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence using the word inundate in the context of a crowded place.
  2. Write a short paragraph describing a situation where someone feels inundated with tasks to complete.
  3. Compose a dialogue between two friends discussing how social media can inundate our lives.

Creative Writing Task:

Imagine you are stranded on a deserted island. Write a story describing how a sudden storm inundated the island, changing everything.

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture of a scene that represents the word inundate to you. Use your imagination to illustrate the concept visually.
