The Word of The Day is FRIVOLOUS 😜

Definition:

Afrivolous person or thing is not serious or sensible, and does not show any care about doing what they should.

Etymology:

The word "frivolous" originated from the Latin word "frivulus", meaning "silly" or "trivial". It entered the English language in the late 15th century.

Example Sentences:

  1. She spent all day on frivolous activities instead of studying for her exam.
  2. His argument was dismissed as frivolous and lacking any substantial evidence.
  3. The judge reprimanded the lawyer for bringing forth a frivolous lawsuit.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence using the word frivolous in reference to a situation in your school.
  2. Write a short paragraph explaining why it is important to focus on meaningful tasks rather than frivolous ones.
  3. Compose a dialogue between two characters discussing the consequences of engaging in frivolous behavior.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem that explores the consequences of a frivolous decision made by the protagonist.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or illustration depicting a frivolous scenario or activity.
