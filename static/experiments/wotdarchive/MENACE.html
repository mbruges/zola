The Word of The Day is 👿 MENACE

Definition:

A menace is something or someone that poses a threat or is likely to cause harm or danger.

Etymology:

The word "menace" originated from the Latin word "minacia," which means "threatening" or "dangerous."

Example Sentences:

  1. He was regarded as a menace to society due to his criminal activities.
  2. The wild animal roaming the neighborhood became a menace to the residents.
  3. The reckless driver on the busy highway was seen as a menace to other motorists.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence using the word menace in a context related to environmental conservation.
  2. Write a short paragraph explaining how to handle a menace in your community.
  3. Compose a dialogue where two characters discuss a potential menace they might face in the future.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (minimum 200 words) using the word menace. Be creative and engage the reader with your narrative.

Drawing Task:

Create a detailed drawing or illustration that represents the concept of menace.
