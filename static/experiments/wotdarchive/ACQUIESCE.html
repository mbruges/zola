The Word of The Day is ACQUIESCE 🙏

Definition: To accept, comply, or submit without objection or resistance.

Etymology: The word comes from the Latin word "acquiescere" which means "to find rest in".

Example Sentences:

  • After a long argument, John finally decided to acquiesce to his wife's decision.
  • The professor refused to acquiesce to the student's request for a grade change.
  • She was tired of fighting the system and decided to acquiesce to the rules.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using acquiesce in the present tense.
  2. Use acquiesce in a sentence with a negative connotation.
  3. Create a question using acquiesce.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story where the main character must acquiesce to a difficult situation, and how they deal with it.

Drawing Task:

Draw a scene where someone is acquiescing to someone else's authority or decision.
