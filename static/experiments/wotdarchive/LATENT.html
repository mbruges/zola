The Word of The Day is LATENT 🤔

Latent means existing but not yet developed or noticeable. It refers to something that is concealed, hidden, or dormant.

Etymology: The word latent originated from the Latin word "latens," which means lying hidden or secret.

Example Sentences:

1. The virus remains latent in the body for a prolonged period before showing symptoms.

2. She had a latent talent for painting, which she discovered only later in life.

3. The detective observed a latent hostility in the suspect's body language.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a sentence using the word latent to describe an undiscovered talent or ability you possess.

2. Use the word latent in a sentence to describe a hidden danger or risk that could arise unexpectedly.

3. Create a sentence featuring the word latent to describe a hidden emotion or feeling someone might have.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story exploring a character who possesses a latent superpower that emerges during a critical moment.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or sketch that represents the concept of something latent or hidden.
