The Word of The Day is MOLLIFY 😀

Mollify is a verb that means to soothe someone's anger or anxiety.

Etymology Summary:

The word "mollify" originated from the Latin word "mollis" meaning soft. It entered the English language in the early 15th century.

Example Sentences:

  1. He tried to mollify his upset friend by offering an apology.
  2. The parent used a calm tone to mollify the crying child.
  3. I brought her favorite sweets to mollify her after our argument.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a paragraph explaining how you would mollify a nervous student before giving a presentation.
  2. Compose a dialogue between two characters where one is trying to mollify the other's anger.
  3. Create a short story illustrating a situation where someone's efforts to mollify a difficult boss backfire.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a letter to a pen pal describing a time when using empathy and understanding helped you mollify a tense situation with a friend or family member.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the act of mollifying another person.
