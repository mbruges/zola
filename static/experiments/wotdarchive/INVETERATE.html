The Word of The Day is INVETERATE 😀

Definition:

A person who has a habit, state, or behavior that is long-established and unlikely to change.

Etymology:

The word "inveterate" traces back to Latin roots, where "in-" means "in" and "vetus" means "old." It entered English in the late 16th century.

Example Sentences:

1. She was an inveterate chocolate lover and couldn't resist a piece after every meal.

2. His inveterate passion for art led him to visit museums around the world.

3. Despite the diet, his inveterate love for ice cream remained unchanged.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a paragraph describing someone you know who has an inveterate habit.

2. Create a conversation between two characters discussing their inveterate behaviors.

3. Write a poem incorporating the word inveterate.

Creative Writing Task:
