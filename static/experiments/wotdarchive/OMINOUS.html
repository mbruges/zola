The Word of The Day is OMINOUS 👻

OMINOUS is an adjective that means giving the impression that something bad or unpleasant is going to happen.

Etymology:

  • From Latin ominosus meaning "full of foreboding"
  • Derived from omen meaning "a sign"

Example Sentences:

1. The dark clouds and thunder were ominous signs of an approaching storm.

2. The detective had an ominous feeling that the murderer was still out there.

3. The sudden silence in the room was ominous, indicating a potential problem.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short story using ominous in at least three different paragraphs.
  2. Write a dialogue between two characters where they discuss an ominous event.
  3. Compose a poem where you explore the theme of ominous and its impact on emotions.

Creative Writing Task:

Imagine you are standing in front of an ominous door. Write a detailed description of the scene and what might be on the other side.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the feeling of ominous. Use colors and shapes to evoke a sense of foreboding and tension.
