The Word of The Day is 😎 CANDOUR

Definition:

Candour refers to the quality of being open and honest in expressing one's thoughts or feelings.

Etymology:

The word "candour" originated from the Latin word "candor," meaning "whiteness" or "bright." It has been used in English since the 14th century.

Example Sentences:

1. She appreciated his candour when he admitted his mistake.

2. The politician's candour about the issue gained public trust.

3. The teacher's candour in providing feedback helps students improve.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a paragraph explaining a situation where candour played a vital role.

2. Create a dialogue between two characters that showcases the use of candour.

3. Write a short story where the main character's candour leads to a positive outcome.

Creative Writing Task:

Write an open-ended story where a group of friends embrace the importance of candour in their daily lives. Let your imagination flow and explore various scenarios highlighting the impact of honesty.

Drawing Task:

Create a detailed drawing that symbolizes the essence of candour. Use your creativity to represent openness and honesty through imagery and design.
