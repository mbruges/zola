The Word of The Day is LAUDABLE 😊

Laudable means deserving praise or commendation.

Etymology: The word "laudable" derives from the Latin word "laudabilis", which means praiseworthy.

Example Sentences:

1. The student's laudable achievements earned him a scholarship.

2. The team's laudable efforts led them to victory.

3. His laudable act of kindness touched many hearts in the community.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a short paragraph describing a laudable accomplishment you have achieved in your life.

2. Create a dialogue between two friends discussing a laudable act they witnessed.

3. Write a poem that celebrates the laudable qualities of a hero or role model.

Open-Ended Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story about a character who embarks on a journey to accomplish a laudable goal against all odds.

Drawing Task:

Create a detailed illustration that depicts a scene portraying a laudable act of courage or selflessness.
