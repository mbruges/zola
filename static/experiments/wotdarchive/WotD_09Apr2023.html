Eclectic (adjective) - deriving ideas, style, or taste from a broad and diverse range of sources.

Etymology: The word "eclectic" comes from the Greek "eklektikos" meaning "selective".

Example sentences:

  1. Her eclectic taste in music ranges from classical to hip hop.
  2. The interior design of the cafe is an eclectic mix of vintage and modern decor.

Practice tasks:

  1. Replace the underlined word with eclectic. Example: His wide taste in music ranges from jazz to rock.
  2. Write a sentence that uses eclectic to describe someone's fashion sense.
  3. Make a list of three things that you enjoy that come from different genres or styles.

Creative writing task: Write a short story about a character who has an eclectic personality, using the word at least twice in your story.

Drawing task: Draw a picture of a scene that is eclectic, using various colors and styles.
