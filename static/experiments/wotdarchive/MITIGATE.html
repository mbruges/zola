The Word of The Day is MITIGATE 😉

Definition:

Mitigate means to make something less severe, serious, or painful.

Etymology:

The word "mitigate" originates from the Latin word "mitigare", meaning "to soften or make mild".

Example Sentences:

1. The doctor prescribed medication to mitigate the patient's pain.

2. Planting trees can help mitigate the effects of air pollution.

3. The timely intervention of the firefighter helped mitigate the damage caused by the fire.

Practice Tasks:

1. Rewrite the following sentence using the word "mitigate": The teacher tried to reduce the students' anxiety before the exam.

2. Use "mitigate" in a sentence to describe how you would alleviate the impacts of climate change in your community.

3. Create a short dialogue between two characters where one advises the other on how to mitigate the risks of a hiking trip.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story where the protagonist discovers a magical object that can mitigate any form of harm or danger. Explore the consequences and challenges they face while trying to use it.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or sketch illustrating a scenario where taking proactive measures can mitigate the negative impacts of natural disasters.
