The Word of The Day is PARAGON 💪

Definition of paragon: a person or thing regarded as a perfect example of a particular quality.

Etymology Summary:

The word "paragon" originates from the late Middle English word "paragon" or "paragoun", which means "a model of excellence or perfection". It entered the English language from the Old Italian word "paragone" that had a similar meaning.

Example Sentences:

  1. She is known as a paragon of virtue.
  2. This painting is a paragon of Renaissance art.
  3. He excels in every sport; he's a true paragon of athleticism.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph describing a person you consider to be a paragon of kindness.
  2. Compose a sentence using the word paragon to describe a beautiful landscape.
  3. Create a dialogue between two characters discussing their admiration for a paragon of intelligence.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story featuring a protagonist who strives to become a paragon in their chosen field, facing numerous challenges along the way.

Drawing Task:

Using pen and paper or any digital drawing tool, create an illustration inspired by the concept of a paragon.
