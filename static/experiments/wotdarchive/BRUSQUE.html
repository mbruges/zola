The Word of the Week is BRUSQUE 😓

BRUSQUE (adjective): abrupt or curt in manner or speech

Etymology: from French brusque, from Italian brusco, meaning rough or tart

Example Sentences:

  1. His brusque reply left her feeling hurt.
  2. The CEO was known for his brusque attitude and short fuse.
  3. She was taken aback by the brusque dismissal of her proposal.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using brusque to describe a character in a book you're reading.
  2. Make up a conversation between two coworkers, one of whom is being brusque.
  3. Describe a scenario where someone's brusque behavior would be appropriate.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or scene where a brusque character learns a valuable lesson about kindness and empathy.

Drawing Task:

Draw a cartoon of a person with a brusque attitude and a person with a kind attitude, facing each other.
