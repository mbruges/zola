The Word of The Day is 🤑 MALINGER

Definition:

MALINGER: (verb) to pretend to be ill, injured, or otherwise unable to work in order to avoid doing something.

Etymology Summary:

The word "malingering" originated from the combining of the Old French word "malingre" meaning "ailing" and the Old High German word "linger≈çn" meaning "to be lazy".

Example Sentences:

  • 1. After being assigned a difficult task, he decided to malinge by pretending to have a severe headache.
  • 2. She always found excuses to malinge whenever there were dirty dishes to be washed.
  • 3. The student was caught malingering during the exam by pretending to have a sudden case of dizziness.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Task 1: Write a short paragraph explaining a situation where someone might be tempted to malinge.
  2. Task 2: Create a dialogue between two friends discussing the implications of malingering in the workplace.
  3. Task 3: Write a short story incorporating the word malingering as a central theme.

Creative Writing Task:

Imagine you are a detective investigating a case where a suspect is accused of malingering to avoid facing the consequences of their actions. Write a detailed narrative describing the investigation and how you uncover the truth about their deception.

Drawing Task:

Create a visually appealing drawing or illustration that visually represents the concept of malingering.
