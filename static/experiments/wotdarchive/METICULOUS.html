The Word of The Day is 🤓 METICULOUS

Definition:

A term used to describe someone who pays great attention to detail and is extremely careful and precise in their work.

Etymology:

The word "meticulous" originates from the Latin word "meticulosus", meaning fearful or timid. It gained its current meaning of extreme attention to detail through the years.

Example Sentences:

  1. He is a meticulous editor, checking every punctuation mark with great care.
  2. She meticulously organized her workspace, ensuring everything was in its proper place.
  3. The meticulous craftsmanship of the handmade pot was evident in its intricate details.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a meticulous timeline highlighting important events in history.
  2. Write a short story using meticulous descriptions to bring the setting to life.
  3. Design a poster that showcases the importance of meticulous planning and organization.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a detailed character profile of a meticulous detective who solves complex cases with their meticulous approach to investigation.

Drawing Task:

Create a visually appealing illustration that portrays the concept of meticulous attention to detail.
