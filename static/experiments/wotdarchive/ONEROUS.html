The Word of The Day is ONEROUS 🤔

ONEROUS: (adjective) involving a great amount of effort, difficulty, or burden.

Etymology: The word "onerous" derives from the Latin word "oner≈çsus" meaning "burdensome" or "troublesome."

Example Sentences:

  1. Carrying such an onerous workload, she struggled to find time for herself.
  2. The company faced financial turmoil due to the onerous debts it had accumulated.
  3. Studying for exams can be onerous, but the effort is worth it in the end.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a paragraph describing an onerous task you have recently completed.
  2. Create a dialogue between two characters discussing an onerous responsibility they need to fulfill.
  3. Compose a short story highlighting the efforts and burdens of an onerous journey.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a poem or a short story that captures the emotional weight and challenges associated with onerous obligations.

Drawing Task:

Draw a visual representation of onerous, symbolizing the weight or difficulty of a burdensome task or responsibility.
