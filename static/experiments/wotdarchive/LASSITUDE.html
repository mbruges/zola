The Word of The Day is 🧹 LASSITUDE

Lassitude is a noun that refers to a state of physical or mental weariness or lack of energy.

Etymologically, the word lassitude comes from the Latin word "lassitudo," meaning "weariness."

Example Sentences:

  • I could feel the overwhelming lassitude after working all day in the sun.
  • The long hike left me with a sense of lassitude that I couldn't shake off.
  • His eyes drooped with a visible sign of lassitude as he listened to the monotonous lecture.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short story using lassitude at least three times.
  2. Create a dialogue between two characters discussing lassitude and its effects.
  3. Compose a poem incorporating the word lassitude into the verses.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a journal entry describing a day in your life when lassitude completely took over. Reflect on its cause, impact, and how you managed to overcome it.

Drawing Task:

Create a visual representation of lassitude using any art medium of your choice.
