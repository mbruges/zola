The Word of The Day is PALATABLE 🥚

Definition:

A term used to describe something that is pleasant or acceptable to the taste or the mind.

Etymology:

The word "palatable" first appeared in the late 17th century, derived from the Latin word "palatus" meaning "pleasing to the taste."

Example Sentences:

1. The cake she baked was incredibly palatable and left everyone wanting more.

2. The professor presented the complex concept in a palatable manner, making it easier for the students to understand.

3. The new restaurant received positive reviews for its palatable menu options and charming ambiance.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1:

Create a sentence using the word palatable in the context of a memorable dining experience.

Task 2:

Write a short paragraph explaining why it is important for public speakers to present their ideas in a palatable way.

Task 3:

Compose a sentence that describes a movie you recently watched, expressing whether you found it palatable or not.

Creative Writing Task:

Drawing Task:
