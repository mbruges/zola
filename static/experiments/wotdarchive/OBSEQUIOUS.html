The Word of The Day is OBSEQUIOUS 🤣

Definition:

Obsequious means overly obedient or submissive, often in a way that is insincere or excessive.

Etymology:

The word "obsequious" originates from the Latin word "obsequiosus," meaning compliant or dutiful.

Example Sentences:

  1. The salesperson greeted the customer with an obsequious smile, hoping to make a good impression.
  2. The politician's obsequious behavior towards powerful individuals undermined his authenticity.
  3. She disliked his obsequious flattery and found it insincere.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence in which you use the word obsequious to describe a character in a book you have read.
  2. Rewrite the following sentence using obsequious instead of another similar word: "The waiter provided excellent service in a servile manner."
  3. Write a short dialogue between two people where one character is being overly obsequious to gain personal benefits.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem using the word obsequious as a central theme or motif. Explore the concept of excessive compliance and its consequences in relationships or society.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or illustration depicting a situation where someone is behaving obsequiously. Use your imagination to portray the emotions and actions of those involved.
