The Word of The Day is CHIDE 😡

Definition:

Chide: To express mild disapproval or scold someone gently.

Etymology:

The word "chide" originated from the Old English word "cƒ´dan," which means to contend or argue. Over time, its meaning evolved into reproaching or expressing disapproval.

Example Sentences:

1. Sarah's mother chided her for not completing her homework on time.

2. The teacher chided the students for talking during the lecture.

3. He chided himself for forgetting to bring his umbrella on a rainy day.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a sentence using the word 'chide' to describe a situation at home.

2. Use the word 'chide' in a sentence to express your teacher's mild disagreement.

3. Create a sentence where you chide yourself for a mistake you made.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (minimum of 100 words) where a character receives a chiding from their best friend and reflects on their actions. Explore their emotions and the impact of the scolding on their relationship.

Drawing Task:

Draw a scene depicting someone being gently chided by a family member or friend.
