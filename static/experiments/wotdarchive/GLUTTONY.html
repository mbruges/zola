The Word of The Day is GLUTTONY 🤥

Definition:

Gluttony is the excessive indulgence or overconsumption of food or drink.

Etymology:

The word gluttony originates from the Latin word "gula," meaning throat or gullet.

Example Sentences:

  • I couldn't resist the temptation and gave in to gluttony at the all-you-can-eat buffet.
  • His constant gluttony caused him to gain weight rapidly.
  • She regretted her gluttony as she felt sick from overeating.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a paragraph describing a situation where you witnessed someone engaging in gluttony.
  2. Compose a dialogue between two friends in which one of them accuses the other of being a glutton.
  3. Create a short story that revolves around the theme of gluttony.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a poem about a character struggling with their personal battle against gluttony.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the negative consequences of gluttony.
