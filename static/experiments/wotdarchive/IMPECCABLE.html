The Word of The Day is IMPECCABLE 👌

IMPECCABLE (adjective) - without any flaw or error.

Etymology: The word "impeccable" is derived from the Latin word "impeccabilis", meaning "not liable to sin". It entered the English language in the mid-16th century.

Example Sentences:

  1. Her manners and behavior are always impeccable.
  2. The chef's attention to detail ensures that every dish is impeccable.
  3. He demanded impeccable honesty from his employees at all times.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using the word impeccable to describe your favorite book.
  2. Create a dialogue between two characters where one compliments the other on their impeccable fashion sense.
  3. Compose a short paragraph praising a musician's impeccable technique.

Open-ended Creative Writing Task:

Write a story where the protagonist is known for their impeccable problem-solving skills. Describe a challenging situation they encounter and how they navigate it flawlessly.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the concept of impeccable. Visualize what this word means to you and illustrate it with your artwork.
