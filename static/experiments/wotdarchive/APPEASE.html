The Word of The Day is APPEASE 😊

Appease means to bring a state of peace or calmness, especially by making concessions or giving into demands.

Etymology: From the Old French word 'apaiser', meaning to pacify or soothe.

Example Sentences:

  1. She tried to appease the crying toddler by offering him a cookie.
  2. The government hoped to appease the protesters by making changes to the controversial law.
  3. The manager tried to appease the angry customer by offering a refund.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using appease to describe how you calm yourself when you are stressed.
  2. Use the word appease to suggest how a company might attempt to address customer complaints.
  3. Write a sentence using appease to describe a situation where someone tries to placate a panicking animal.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story about a character who must appease a powerful leader to prevent a war from breaking out.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the concept of appease.
