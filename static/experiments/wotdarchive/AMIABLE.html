The Word of The Day is AMIABLE 😊

Amiable, an adjective, means having or displaying a friendly and pleasant manner. It can also refer to an agreeable disposition or character.

The word "Amiable" originated in the 14th century from the Latin word amicabilis meaning friendly, kind, or pleasant.

Example Sentences:

  1. Amiable Bob greeted everyone with a smile as he walked into the room.
  2. The cute, amiable puppy wagged its tail excitedly as it met the children at the park.
  3. The hostess at the restaurant was always amiable and made sure that her guests felt welcome.

Practice Tasks:

  • Write a sentence using amiable to describe someone you admire.
  • Use the word amiable in a sentence to describe the weather.
  • Unscramble the word EGAILMA and write a sentence using the word once you've figured it out.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story about a character who is known for their amiable nature. What challenges do they face because of this? How do they overcome them?

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture of a person or an animal with an amiable expression on their face. This person/animal should be surrounded by other people/animals who also look happy and friendly.
