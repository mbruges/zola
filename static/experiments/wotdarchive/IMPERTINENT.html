The Word of The Day is IMPERTINENT 🙄

Definition:

Impertinent (adj.): not showing proper respect; rude or disrespectful.

Etymology:

The word "impertinent" originated from the Latin word "impertinens," which means "not belonging." It entered the English language in the late 14th century.

Example Sentences:

  • He was scolded for his impertinent remarks during the meeting.
  • The waiter's impertinent behavior towards the customers was unacceptable.
  • She couldn't tolerate his impertinent questions about her personal life.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph using the word impertinent correctly in context.
  2. Create a dialogue between two characters where one of them behaves impertinently.
  3. Compose a poem or song lyrics that incorporate the word impertinent.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story that begins with the line, "The sound of laughter echoed through the classroom, but one student's impertinent comment changed everything."

Drawing Task:

Create a detailed illustration that represents the concept of impertinence in a visually creative way.
