The Word of The Day is VERNACULAR üó£Ô∏è

Definition:

Vernacular refers to the language or dialect spoken by the people who live in a particular region or country, especially as distinguished from a literary language or a foreign language.

Etymology:

The word vernacular comes from the Latin vernaculus, meaning "domestic, native."

Examples:

  • She was fluent in the vernacular of the street.
  • The author's use of vernacular made the dialogue feel more authentic.

Practice:

  1. Use vernacular in a sentence of your own.
  2. Write a paragraph in your own vernacular, describing your favorite food.
  3. Identify five words that are commonly used in your vernacular and explain what they mean.

Creative Writing:

Write a short story using as much vernacular as possible. Be creative!

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents your vernacular in some way. This could be a depiction of your hometown, your friends and family, or any other aspect of your life that reflects the way you speak.
