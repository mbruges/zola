The Word of The Day is 😔 MELANCHOLY

Definition: The state of being sad and feeling a deep, pensive sadness.

Etymology Summary: The word "melancholy" has roots in Greek and Latin. In Greek, the word "melankholia" meant "black bile," one of the four bodily humors. In Latin, "melancholia" became associated with a condition characterized by a sad mood or disposition.

Example Sentences:

  1. I woke up feeling a deep sense of melancholy today.
  2. She retreated to her room, enveloped in a cloud of melancholy.
  3. The haunting melody of the piano played in the background amplified the melancholy atmosphere of the room.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1:

Task 2:

Task 3:

Creative Writing Task:
