The Word of The Day is OBLIQUE 😎

Oblique is defined as slanting or sloping; not straightforward or direct.

Etymology: The word "oblique" comes from the late Middle English word "oblique," which originated from the Latin word "obliquus," meaning "slanting."

Example Sentences:

  • She gave him an oblique look before walking away.
  • The detective asked oblique questions to avoid revealing too much.
  • The car crashed into an oblique position after swerving off the road.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using the word oblique to describe a picture you select.
  2. Create a conversation between two characters where one character responds obliquely to the other's question.
  3. Write a short paragraph where you explain a concept using oblique language and make the reader figure it out.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story about a character who takes an oblique path in life, encountering unexpected adventures along the way.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the concept of oblique. It could be a figure standing at a tilted angle or any visual representation that captures the idea of not being straightforward.
