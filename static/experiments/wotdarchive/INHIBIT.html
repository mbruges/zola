The Word of The Day is INHIBIT 🙅

INHIBIT is a verb which means to restrain, hinder, or prevent something from happening or developing.

Etymology: The word "inhibit" comes from the Latin word "inhibere", derived from "in-" (meaning not) and "habere" (meaning to have), which gives the sense of holding something back.

Example Sentences:

  1. His fear of failure inhibited him from pursuing his dreams.
  2. The presence of a strict teacher can inhibit students from expressing their true creativity.
  3. The cold weather will inhibit the growth of most plants.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph describing a situation where fear inhibits someone's ability to succeed.
  2. Compose a dialogue where one character encourages another to overcome their inhibitions and try something new.
  3. Create a list of strategies that can help individuals overcome inhibitions and become more confident in public speaking.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story about a young musician who initially inhibits their own talents due to self-doubt and how they eventually find the courage to share their music with the world.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the feeling of breaking through personal inhibitions and embracing one's true self.
