The Word of The Day is PARADOX ü§î

Definition:

A statement or situation that contradicts itself, yet may be true or have a valid explanation.

Etymology:

The word "paradox" originated from the Greek word "paradoxos", meaning "contrary to expectations, incredible".

Example Sentences:

Paradox: This statement is false.

Paradox: The only constant in life is change.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write down two paradoxical statements.
  2. Explain what makes a statement a paradox.
  3. Can you think of a paradox that is not a statement?

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story that involves a paradox as a central plot point.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or illustration that represents a paradox.
