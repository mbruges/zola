The Word of The Day is INHERENT 💡

Definition:

Inherent means existing as a quality or characteristic that is permanent or inseparable, naturally belonging to something or someone.

Etymology:

The word "inherent" originated from the Latin word "inhaerēre" which means "to stick or adhere to".

Example Sentences:

  1. The inherent beauty of nature never fails to amaze me.
  2. Trust is an inherent quality in any healthy relationship.
  3. He showed an inherent talent for playing the piano at a young age.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create three sentences using the word inherent to describe different qualities in your favorite animal.
  2. Write a short paragraph discussing the inherent challenges faced by scientists in the pursuit of new discoveries.
  3. Compose a poem highlighting the inherent beauty of a sunset.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (at least 300 words) where the concept of inherent characteristics plays a central role in the plot development.

Drawing Task:

Create a detailed sketch showcasing the inherent strengths of a superhero of your own creation.
