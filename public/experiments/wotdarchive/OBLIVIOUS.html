The Word of The Day is 🤔 OBLIVIOUS

Definition:

Oblivious means unaware or forgetful of something.

Etymology:

The word "oblivious" originated from the Latin word "obliviosus," which means forgetful or heedless.

Example Sentences:

1. She was so oblivious to her surroundings that she walked into a pole.

2. The teacher was so loud that the students remained oblivious to the storm outside.

3. He was completely oblivious to the fact that everyone was planning a surprise party for him.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1: Write a sentence using the word oblivious to describe a situation where someone is completely unaware of their surroundings.

Task 2: Rewrite the following sentence, replacing the underlined word with oblivious: "She was so absorbed in her book that she didn't notice the time passing."

Task 3: Fill in the blank with the appropriate form of oblivious: "Despite the noise around him, he remained _________ to everything happening."

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or paragraph using the word oblivious. Imagine a character who is always oblivious to the funny situations happening around them.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the concept of being oblivious. Use your creativity to depict a scene where someone is unaware of their surroundings or a funny moment of obliviousness.
