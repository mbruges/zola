The Word of The Day is MALLEABLE 📘

Definition:

MALLEABLE: (adjective) capable of being shaped or bent without breaking.

Etymology:

The word "malleable" comes from the Latin word "malleus" meaning "hammer".

Example Sentences:

1. The sculptor used a mallet to shape the malleable clay.

2. The chef demonstrated how to make malleable dough for breadmaking.

3. The artist displayed his malleable artwork, which could be reshaped by the viewer.

Practice Tasks:

1. Use the word "malleable" in a sentence describing a material that can be easily molded.

2. Write a short paragraph discussing the advantages of having a malleable mindset when facing challenges.

3. Create a dialogue between two characters where one accuses the other of being malleable in their beliefs.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story about a malleable object that possesses magical properties. How does its malleability affect the story?

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing depicting a malleable substance being shaped or molded into something interesting.
