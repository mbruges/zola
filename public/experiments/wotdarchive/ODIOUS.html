The Word of The Day is ODIOUS 😖

Odious means extremely unpleasant, repulsive, or deserving of hatred.

It is derived from the Latin word "odiosus," which means hateful or offensive.

Example sentences:

  1. The dictator's oppressive regime was widely considered to be odious by the citizens.
  2. She couldn't bear the odious smell coming from the trash bin.
  3. Despite his charming appearance, the politician had an odious reputation for corruption.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence using the word odious in the context of describing someone's behavior.
  2. Write a short paragraph using odious to describe a place or situation you strongly dislike.
  3. Compose a dialogue between two characters using the word odious to express their feelings towards each other.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story or poem that includes the word odious and explores the theme of overcoming adversity.

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture that represents the concept of odious in any way you interpret it.
