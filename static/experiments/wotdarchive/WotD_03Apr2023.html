The Word of The Day is BENEVOLENCE 🌺

Benevolence is the act of being kind and generous towards others. It is an attitude of goodwill and a desire to do good.

Etymology: The word ‘benevolence’ comes from the Latin words ‘bene’ meaning good, and ‘volens’ meaning willing.

Example sentences:

  • She showed great benevolence by donating a large sum of money to the charity.
  • It was clear that the company acted out of benevolence when they offered to pay for their employees' health insurance.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a sentence using the word benevolence.
  2. Think of three ways you can show benevolence to someone today.
  3. Discuss with a partner the importance of benevolence in our daily lives.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story about a person who shows benevolence to a stranger, and how it affects both of their lives.


Drawing Task:

Draw a picture of a person showing benevolence to someone else.
