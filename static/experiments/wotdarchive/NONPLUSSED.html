The Word of The Day is NONPLUSSED 😮

Definition:

A state of perplexity or confusion; unable to understand or make sense of something.

Etymology:

The word "nonplussed" comes from the Latin word "non plus" meaning "no more." It originally referred to a state of being at a loss or unable to proceed further.

Example Sentences:

1. Sarah was nonplussed when she realized she had lost her keys.

2. The complicated math problem left the students feeling nonplussed.

3. The unexpected plot twist in the movie left the audience nonplussed.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a short story using the word "nonplussed" at least twice.

2. Create a dialogue between two characters where one of them is nonplussed by the other's statement.

3. Write a poem incorporating the feeling of being nonplussed.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a reflection piece on a time when you felt completely nonplussed, exploring the reasons behind your confusion and how you eventually overcame it.

Drawing Task:

Create an artistic representation of the word "nonplussed" using any medium you prefer. Let your creativity shine!
