The Word of The Day is FORBEARANCE 🙌

Definition:

Forbearance is the act of showing self-control, patience, and restraint in the face of provocation, or when dealing with difficult situations or people.

Etymology:

The word "forbearance" derives from the Old English word "forberan," meaning "to endure, bear patiently." It is a combination of the prefix "for-" (meaning "intensive") and "beran" (meaning "to bear").

Example Sentences:

1. Despite the rude comments, she showed forbearance and responded with kindness.

2. The teacher demonstrated forbearance when the students were being disruptive, giving them a chance to calm down.

3. It takes a great deal of forbearance to remain calm in traffic during rush hour.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1: Write a sentence describing a situation where you demonstrated forbearance.

Task 2: Create a short dialogue between two characters exemplifying forbearance.

Task 3: Write a paragraph explaining how forbearance can positively impact relationships with others.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story using forbearance as a central theme. Explore how forbearance can lead to personal growth and resolve conflicts.

Drawing Task:

Create an original drawing or artwork that represents the concept of forbearance.
