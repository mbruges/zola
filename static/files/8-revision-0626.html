Suggested Techniques and Effects (your answers may vary slightly)

1. "The thing had taken another lamb in the night, as expected."

Technique: Short sentence / understatement

Effect: Creates matter-of-fact tone; emphasizes the creature's predictable, relentless nature.

2. "climbing the stark rock-face, ribbed with clefts and riddled with caves, where anything could hide"

Technique: Imagery and personification

Effect: Vivid sensory description; the rock-face is described as if it has human features, creating an ominous, forbidding landscape.

3. "the creature had been dragging its kill, obliterating its footprints as it made them"

Technique: Verb choice (obliterating)

Effect: Violent, destructive verb choice emphasizes the creature's raw, unthinking nature and creates a sense of horror.

4. "Then in a snow-filled cranny made filthy with blood"

Technique: Contrast and imagery

Effect: Juxtaposition of pure white snow with violent red blood creates visual impact and revulsion.

5. "now the trail was marked with a line of blood drops"

Technique: Imagery

Effect: Visual detail makes the hunt visceral and real; suggests the creature is wounded or the trail is fresh and urgent.

6. "Like a hare it left leg-marks as well as footprints from time to time"

Technique: Simile

Effect: Compares the creature's movement to an animal, deepening the mystery and suggesting it moves unnaturally.

7. "They shook their heads and moved on."

Technique: Short sentence / understatement

Effect: Creates a sense of unease and acceptance; tension builds through simplicity.

8. "The finely balanced mixture of excitement and fear which had driven them urgently till that moment shifted suddenly towards fear."

Technique: Abstract noun and tone shift

Effect: Long sentence reflects the shepherds' emotional state; the shift to fear builds tension and signals a change in the narrative.

9. "They could hear it snarling and growling."

Technique: Sensory detail (sound) and onomatopoeia

Effect: Auditory imagery creates immediacy and dread; animal sounds dehumanize the creature.

10. "The melt-cave at the door of the rock cave was abominable with scraps of slaughtered things—with blood and feathers and bones."

Technique: List and word choice (abominable)

Effect: Accumulation of grotesque details; the word 'abominable' is judgmental and creates disgust; the em-dash adds emphasis.

11. "Even on the nearly frozen air, a stench reached them."

Technique: Sensory detail (smell)

Effect: Makes the horror tangible and immediate; appeals to reader's sense of revulsion.

12. "snarling like a dog as it ate"

Technique: Simile

Effect: Compares the creature to an animal, reinforcing its wild, untamed nature.

13. "He jumped forward with a wail and, grabbing Galceran's sleeve, deflected the blow."

Technique: Action and exclamation (wail)

Effect: Short, urgent verbs create drama and show panic; the wail is an emotional sound that signals something has changed.

14. "It ran away on all fours, zigzag in the spaces left by the line of men."

Technique: Imagery and movement

Effect: 'Zigzag' creates a visual pattern of frantic, uncontrolled movement; emphasizes the creature's desperation and animal nature.

15. "It ran on the bent back knuckles of its hand and on bent forward toes."

Technique: Precise physical imagery

Effect: Detailed, grotesque description creates a powerful visual of how alien and wrong this creature is; builds horror through specificity.

Activity 2: Creative Writing Task

You have two choices. Select ONE of the prompts below and complete the planning box and writing task.

Prompt A: Continue the Story

The extract ends with the shepherds' decision about the feral child. Write what happens next. You could explore:

  • Do they take the child back to civilisation?
  • What challenges do they face?
  • How does the child respond?
  • How do the other villagers react?
  • What is the child's fate?

Prompt B: The Child's Perspective

Rewrite a key moment from the extract from the feral child's point of view. You could choose:

  • The moment it first encounters the hunters
  • The moment it is captured
  • An earlier moment in its life (e.g. memories of being raised by wolves, or the moment it killed its first lamb)
  • Show us what the child thinks and feels

Planning Box

Before you write, complete the following:

Setting: Where and when is your scene set? What does it look like, smell like, sound like?
Character(s): Who is in your scene? What does the feral child want or need at this moment?
Atmosphere/Mood: What feeling do you want to create? (e.g. tension, pity, terror, mystery, sadness)
Key Techniques: Which writer's techniques will you use? (e.g. sensory language, short sentences for effect, dialogue, contrast, simile/metaphor, varied sentence structures)
Opening Sentence Idea: Write a hook that will grab your reader's attention.
