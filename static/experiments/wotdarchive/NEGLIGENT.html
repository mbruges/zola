😥 The Word of The Day is NEGLIGENT 😥

Definition:

A person or action that shows lack of care or attention; marked by carelessness or neglect.

Etymology Summary:

The word "negligent" comes from the Latin word "negligentem" meaning "not to pick up, disregard." It entered the English language in the 16th century.

Example Sentences:

  • The student was negligent in completing their homework, and received a low grade.
  • The company was found guilty of negligent behavior for failing to provide a safe working environment.
  • The driver caused an accident due to their negligent actions on the road.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph describing a scenario where someone acted negligently and explain its consequences.
  2. Create a dialogue between two characters discussing a situation where one of them was negligent and how it affected the other person.
  3. Compose a poem that highlights the importance of being attentive and avoiding negligence in daily life.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story using the word negligent. Explore its impact on the characters and how it changes their lives.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the concept of negligence visually. Use your imagination and be creative!
