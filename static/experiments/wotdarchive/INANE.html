The Word of The Day is INANE 🤩

Definition:

An adjective meaning silly, senseless, or lacking sense or meaning.

Etymology:

The word "inane" originated in the late Middle English period from the Latin word "inanis," which also means empty or vacuous.

Example Sentences:

  1. His inane jokes made everyone roll their eyes.
  2. She found the conversation utterly inane and decided to leave.
  3. The movie's plot was so inane that it was difficult to sit through.

Practice Tasks:

Task 1: Write a sentence using the word inane to describe a situation you find amusing.

Task 2: Rewrite the following sentence, replacing the underlined word with inane: "The professor's lecture was extremely boring."

Task 3: Create a short dialogue between two characters using the word inane to highlight their foolish conversation.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (minimum 200 words) where the main character embarks on a journey to find the most inane object in the world. Be creative!

Drawing Task:

Create a colorful drawing that represents the concept of inane. Use your imagination to depict a scene that exemplifies the senselessness or absurdity of something.
