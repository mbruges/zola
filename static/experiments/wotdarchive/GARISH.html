The Word of The Day is GARISH 🧡

GARISH: excessively bright or showy, often in a tasteless or vulgar way.

Etymology: The word "garish" originated from the Middle English word "gawwaris" meaning "ostentatious" or "showy." It has evolved from the Old French word "garric" meaning "warlike" or "bold."

Example Sentences:

  1. She wore a garish outfit with neon colors that blinded everyone.
  2. The hotel lobby was filled with garish decorations and bright lights.
  3. His writing style was often descriptive, but sometimes it became unnecessarily garish.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create your own sentence using the word garish.
  2. Write a short paragraph explaining why garish designs might not be suitable for formal occasions.
  3. Identify three objects or scenarios that you think could be described as garish and explain why.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (at least 200 words) that includes a character who loves everything garish. Explore their unique perspective and how it affects their interactions with others and their environment.

Drawing Task:

Create a colorful and garish drawing depicting your own interpretation of the word.
