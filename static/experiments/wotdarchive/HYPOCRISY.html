The Word of The Day is HYPOCRISY üé≠

Definition:

The word HYPOCRISY refers to the practice of claiming to have moral standards or beliefs that one's own behavior does not align with.

Etymology:

The term originates from the late Middle English word hypocrisie which comes from the Old French ypocrisie. This, in turn, has roots in the late Latin hypocrisis meaning "a play-actor" or "hypocrisy".

Example Sentences:

  1. John condemned others for not recycling, yet he didn't recycle himself, revealing his hypocrisy.
  2. The politician's speeches about honesty were seen as nothing more than hypocrisy by the public.
  3. Samantha always preached about the importance of punctuality, but her frequent tardiness exposed her hypocrisy.

Practice Tasks:

Use HYPOCRISY in the following tasks:

  1. Create a sentence that describes a situation involving hypocrisy at your school.
  2. Write a paragraph explaining why hypocrisy is a harmful behavior.
  3. Compose a short dialogue in which two characters discuss someone's hypocrisy.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story emphasizing the negative consequences of hypocrisy in society. Be imaginative and explore different scenarios!

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that visually represents the concept of hypocrisy. You can use symbols, imagery, or characters to convey your message.
