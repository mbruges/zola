The Word of The Day is LOITER 😀

LOITER: to stand or wait around without any purpose or act slowly.

Etymology: The word "loiter" originated from the 14th century Middle English word "loiteren," which means to linger or idle. It ultimately derived from the Middle Low German word "lotern," which means to move slowly or stand aimlessly.

Example Sentences:

  1. Loitering outside the shop can make the owner suspicious.
  2. The security guard told the teenagers not to loiter near the entrance.
  3. It's important not to loiter when you are running late.

Practice Tasks:

  • Loiter near the park bench and write down what you observe for 10 minutes.
  • Write a sentence using the word loiter in the present continuous tense.
  • Create a dialogue between two characters where one accuses the other of loitering.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story (at least 200 words) where the protagonist's life changes after witnessing a mysterious person loitering in their neighborhood.

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture of people loitering in a public space.
