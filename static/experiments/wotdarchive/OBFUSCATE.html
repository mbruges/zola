The Word of The Day is OBFUSCATE 🔥

Definition:

Obfuscate means to make something unclear, confusing, or difficult to understand.

Etymology Summary:

The word obfuscate comes from the Latin word "obfuscatus," which means "darkened" or "obscured." It is derived from the combination of "ob" meaning "in front of" and "fuscus" meaning "dark."

Example Sentences:

1. The politician obfuscated the issue by giving vague and contradictory answers.

2. The witness tried to obfuscate the facts of the case during cross-examination.

3. The complex legal jargon in the contract was deliberately obfuscated to confuse the average reader.

Practice Tasks:

1. Rewrite the following sentence using the word "obfuscate": "The teacher explained the concept clearly to help us understand."

2. Fill in the blank with the correct form of the word "obfuscate": "The company's confusing advertisement ________ the true costs of their product."

3. Create a sentence using the word "obfuscate" to describe a situation you have experienced.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story in which a character intentionally uses obfuscation to achieve their goals. Consider the motivations and consequences of their actions.

Drawing Task:

Create a visual representation of the word "obfuscate." This could be a drawing, painting, or any other form of visual art that represents the concept of confusion or obscurity.
