The Word of The Day is ENMITY 🤠

Enmity means the state or feeling of being actively opposed or hostile to someone or something.

Derived from the Middle English word "enmite," from Old French "enemistie," from Latin "inimicitia," from "inimicus" meaning "hostile," the word was first used in the 15th century.

Example sentences:

  1. There is a lot of enmity between the two rival gangs in the city.
  2. She felt intense enmity towards her former employer after being unfairly fired.
  3. The enmity between the two nations lasted for decades.

Practice Tasks:

  • Write a sentence using enmity to describe a situation in your school or workplace
  • Create a dialogue between two people who are experiencing enmity towards each other
  • Use enmity in a creative insult directed towards a fictional character or celebrity

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story or poem that features the theme of enmity.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or illustration that represents the feeling of enmity.
