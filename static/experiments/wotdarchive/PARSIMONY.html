The Word of The Day is PARSIMONY 🧐

Definition:

A parsimony refers to the quality of being unwilling to spend money or resources; extreme frugality or stinginess.

Etymology Summary:

The word parsimony derives from the Latin word "parsimonia," meaning frugality or thrift. It entered the English language in the early 16th century.

Example Sentences:

  • Due to his parsimony, John rarely treated himself to anything beyond the bare necessities.

  • The company's success is credited to the CEO's parsimony in managing expenses.

  • Her extreme parsimony could be interpreted as being cheap rather than thrifty.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Create a sentence using the word parsimony to describe a person's financial behavior.

  2. Write a short dialogue where two characters discuss the advantages and disadvantages of parsimony.

  3. Compose a paragraph that highlights the importance of parsimony in social or environmental contexts.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a story that revolves around a character who struggles with parsimony and eventually learns the value of generosity.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing or illustration that represents the concept of parsimony visually.
