The Word of The Day is 🤷 INDOLENT

Definition:

An indolent person is disinclined to work or exert any effort; lazy or idle.

Etymology:

The word "indolent" originates from the Latin word "indolens" meaning "free from pain" or "insensitive."

Example Sentences:

1. The indolent cat laid lazily in the sun all day.

2. Mary's indolent behavior caused her to miss several deadlines.

3. The indolent student didn't bother studying for the exam.

Practice Tasks:

1. Write a paragraph describing a typical day in the life of an indolent person.

2. Compose a sentence using the word indolent to describe a character in a book you recently read.

3. Write a short dialogue between two friends discussing how to help one of them overcome their indolence.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story where the main protagonist is an indolent individual who learns the importance of hard work.

Drawing Task:

Create a drawing that represents the concept of indolence.
