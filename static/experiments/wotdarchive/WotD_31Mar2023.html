The Word of the Week is ECLECTIC üåü

Eclectic (adjective): selecting or employing individual elements from a variety of sources, systems, or styles.

Etymology: The word "eclectic" originated from the Greek word "eklektikos" which means "selective". It was first used in English during the 17th century.

Example sentences:

  1. Jane has an eclectic taste in music. She likes classical, rock, and hip-hop.
  2. The interior design of the caf√© is eclectic, with a mix of vintage and modern elements.

Practice tasks:

  1. Find 5 examples of eclectic art styles and write them down.
  2. Name 3 public figures who are known for their eclectic fashion sense.
  3. Think of 2 other words that have a similar meaning to eclectic.

Creative writing task:

Write a short story about a person who lives an eclectic lifestyle - they have an eclectic taste in music, fashion, and food. How do their choices reflect their personality? How do others perceive them?

Drawing task:

Draw a picture of an eclectic room - it could be a living room, bedroom, or even a workspace. Incorporate different elements from various styles and sources.
