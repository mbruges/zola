The Word of The Day is MYRIAD 🛠

Myriad means a countless or extremely large number of people or things.

The word myriad comes from the Greek word "murias," meaning "ten thousand."

Example Sentences

1. The myriad stars twinkled in the night sky.

2. She had a myriad of ideas for her new startup.

3. The city's myriad problems seem insurmountable.

Practice Tasks

1. Write a sentence using the word myriad to describe a busy street corner.

2. List a myriad of ways to reduce your carbon footprint.

3. Describe a person who has a myriad of talents.

Creative Writing Task

Write a short story about a character who discovers a myriad of surprises on a trip to a new city.

Drawing Task

Draw a picture of a myriad of animals in a forest.
