The Word of The Day is MISANTHROPE

Definition:

A misanthrope refers to someone who dislikes or avoids human society. It is often associated with having a general distrust or hatred towards humanity.

Etymology:

The term misanthrope originates from the Greek words "misos" meaning "hatred" and "anthropos" meaning "human."

Example Sentences:

  1. John, a known misanthrope, rarely attends social gatherings.
  2. The author's main character in the novel is portrayed as a misanthrope, isolated from society.
  3. As a self-proclaimed misanthrope, Rebecca preferred spending time alone.

Practice Tasks:

  1. Write a short paragraph about a fictional character who is a misanthrope.
  2. Create a dialogue between two characters, one of whom is a misanthrope.
  3. Incorporate the word misanthrope into a sentence that describes your opinion on modern society.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story where the protagonist, a misanthrope, learns to appreciate the beauty of human connections.

Drawing Task:

Create a visual representation of a misanthrope using your preferred artistic style.
