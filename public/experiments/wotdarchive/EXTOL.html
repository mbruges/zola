The Word of The Day is EXTOL 👍

EXTOL: to praise enthusiastically.

Etymology: from Latin "extollere" meaning "to elevate", which later evolved to "extollere" meaning "to praise highly".

Example Sentences:

  1. I always extol the virtues of hard work to my children.
  2. The coach would always extol his team's efforts after every game.
  3. The book was extolled by many critics for its gripping plot and vivid descriptions.

Practice Tasks:

  • Write a short paragraph describing a person or a thing you extol.
  • Give a speech about a topic that you extol.
  • Make a list of five things you can extol about yourself or someone else.

Creative Writing Task:

Write a short story about a character who discovers the power of extolling the people in their life.

Drawing Task:

Draw a picture that conveys the feeling of extolling someone or something.
